early life

Mahendra Singh Dhoni was born on 7 July 1981 in Ranchi, Jharkhand, India. He grew up in a middle-class family and showed an early interest in sports, particularly cricket and football. Dhoni's father, Pan Singh, worked in junior management positions in MECON, while his mother, Devaki Devi, is a homemaker.

career

Dhoni made his debut for the Indian national team in 2004 and quickly established himself as a key player. He became the captain of the Indian cricket team in 2007 and led the team to numerous victories, including the ICC T20 World Cup in 2007, the ICC Cricket World Cup in 2011, and the ICC Champions Trophy in 2013.

legacy

Dhoni is widely regarded as one of the greatest captains in the history of cricket. His calm demeanor, strategic thinking, and ability to perform under pressure have earned him the nickname "Captain Cool." He retired from international cricket in August 2020 but continues to play in the Indian Premier League (IPL) for Chennai Super Kings.

personal life

Dhoni is married to Sakshi Singh Rawat, and they have a daughter named Ziva. He is known for his philanthropic efforts and has been involved in various charitable activities, including supporting education and healthcare initiatives in India.
